# ANTS — Visual Identity Brief

*A brief for designers. Intended to commission a logo and basic visual identity system for an open-source distributed-AI protocol.*

---

## 1 · The project

**ANTS** is an open peer-to-peer protocol that allows millions of machines — laptops, phones, servers, GPU farms — to contribute to and draw from a shared, sovereign, verifiable artificial intelligence. There is no company behind it, no token, no central server. Every peer that joins the network contributes work (compute, storage, bandwidth, verification, attention) and, in proportion to its contribution, draws inference from the colony.

The name comes from the foundational observation: no single ant in a colony is intelligent. Each follows simple rules. The colony — taken as a whole — builds cities, farms, wages wars, remembers. Intelligence here is not a property of the parts. It emerges, irreducibly, from the participation of all. ANTS proposes to do the same thing with software.

The project lives at **[ants.community](https://ants.community)** and on GitHub at **[github.com/Ants-Community](https://github.com/Ants-Community)**. The full manifesto is in the main repository.

## 2 · Brand personality

The brand should feel:

- **Pragmatic, not romantic.** This is engineering for a serious problem, not poetry. Confidence comes from craft, not from slogans.
- **Open, not corporate.** No glossy startup energy. No "we're disrupting AI" energy. More like Linux, Matrix, Tor — calmly authoritative.
- **Cypherpunk-adjacent, but not aggressive.** Sympathetic to privacy and decentralisation, but the message is "build" not "fight".
- **European, not Silicon Valley.** Originated in Italy, sober rather than loud, restraint over spectacle.
- **Permanent, not trendy.** This is a protocol designed to outlive its founders. The mark should feel like it has always existed.

If we were a magazine cover, we would be *Wallpaper* or *Domus*, not *Wired* or *Fast Company*.

## 3 · What we need

Primary deliverable is a **mark** (logo symbol) that can stand alone. Secondary deliverables are a **wordmark** ("ANTS" set in a chosen typeface) and a **lockup** (mark + wordmark together). The mark must work without the wordmark, because at small sizes (favicon, app icon) the wordmark disappears.

Full deliverables list:

1. Primary mark — master vector (SVG), monochrome
2. Wordmark — typographic treatment of "ANTS"
3. Lockup — horizontal and stacked variants
4. Favicon — explicitly designed for 16×16 and 32×32 px, not just a scaled-down mark
5. Social avatar — works inside a circular crop
6. Open Graph image — 1200×630, for social previews
7. Source files (Figma preferred, or Illustrator)
8. Brief brand guidelines document (1–2 pages): clear-space rules, minimum sizes, do/don't examples, colour values

Export formats: SVG (master), PNG at 16/32/64/128/256/512/1024/2048, PDF for print.

## 4 · Visual direction

We have a clear sense of what we want and what we don't. The designer has full creative freedom within these bounds.

### What we are drawn to

- **Plural over singular.** A single ant figure is wrong by definition; the project is named after the colony. The mark should encode multiplicity, even abstractly.
- **Stigmergy as a possible conceptual anchor.** Ants coordinate through *traces* (pheromone trails), not through commands. The trail is the protocol; the ants are its current carriers. A mark that suggests *trace, path, or procession* would be philosophically coherent — but is not mandatory.
- **Geometric over illustrative.** Construction lines, mathematical proportions, mark-making rather than drawing.
- **Restraint.** Negative space as a first-class element. Nothing decorative. Nothing that could not be defended in a critique.
- **Black-and-white first.** Colour, if present, should be secondary. The mark must survive in single-ink print, in embroidery, on stickers, on a black background.

### Logos we admire (for tone, not to imitate)

- **Matrix** — the protocol logo: geometric, neutral, calmly serious
- **Tor Project** — the onion mark: distinctive without being decorative
- **IPFS** — the lattice mark: directly encodes the protocol's structure
- **Signal** — the speech-bubble mark: simple, memorable, unmistakable
- **Tailscale** — the keyhole-and-mesh mark: technical without being cold
- **The Internet Archive** — the column mark: institutional gravity, no marketing
- **Apache Foundation** — calm institutional design across decades

### What we do not want

- Anthropomorphic or cartoon ants. No characters. No cuteness. No personality.
- AI-startup gradient blobs, glowing orbs, "intelligence" auras.
- Generic tech tropes: circuit boards, neurons, brain icons, gears, infinity loops.
- Loading-spinner aesthetics (8 equally-spaced dots on a circle reads as "loading").
- 3D rendering, depth effects, photorealism.
- NFT/Web3 colourful chaos.
- Anything reminiscent of **NATS** (the unrelated messaging protocol — phonetically and conceptually adjacent, must remain visually distinct).

## 5 · Usage contexts

The mark will appear in:

- GitHub organisation avatar (square, sometimes circular-cropped)
- X / BlueSky / Mastodon profile pictures (circular)
- Documentation site header
- Conference slide decks (must read at small size on the bottom of slides)
- Stickers (single-colour, laser-cut compatible)
- Embroidered patches (no thin lines, no fine detail)
- Printed papers and reports (1-colour and 2-colour print)
- T-shirts and merch (1-colour silkscreen)
- Possibly: an installer screen, a CLI banner (ASCII representation should be conceivable)

A successful mark survives each of these contexts without modification.

---

## 6 · Constraints

- Must work in pure monochrome (black on white **and** white on black)
- Must remain legible at 16×16 px (the favicon test is non-negotiable)
- Must survive single-colour silkscreen and embroidery (no gradients, no halftones, no thin hairlines)
- Must not require explanation (a viewer should "get it" within two seconds)
- Must be entirely original (no stock elements, no derived geometry from existing marks)

## 7 · Current placeholder (for context, not for reference)

We currently use a small placeholder mark generated internally — a procession of geometric chevrons along an implied diagonal trail. It is *not* meant as a starting point. The designer should feel free to disregard it entirely. We include it only so the designer understands the conceptual territory we have explored: trail, procession, stigmergy, plurality, void as protagonist.

The placeholder lives at `/logo.svg` in the main repository.

---

## 8 · Process

Suggested rounds:

- **Round 1**: 3–5 distinct directions, sketch-quality, monochrome. We pick one direction.
- **Round 2**: Refinement of chosen direction; explore proportions, weight, scale behaviour.
- **Round 3**: Final mark, wordmark, lockup, exports, mini-guidelines.

Timeline: 4–6 weeks total, with a working session at the start to align on direction.

---

## 9 · Budget

This is an open-source project, not a venture-funded company. Budget reflects that — but we want to commission a designer who takes the work seriously, not the cheapest option.

Indicative range: **€2,000 – €5,000** for the full scope above. Higher end for a designer with notable protocol/foundation work in their portfolio. Open to discussion.

---

## 10 · Practical

- The mark and all derivatives will be released under **CC0** or **CC BY-SA 4.0** (to be agreed). The project is open-source; the visual identity is part of the commons.
- The designer's credit will be permanently displayed in the project's `BRAND.md` and in any released brand guidelines.
- We are not looking for a single asset; we are looking for a relationship with a designer who can grow the identity over time as the project matures.
